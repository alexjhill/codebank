Caesar Cypher

#  History

The Caesar cypher is named after Julius Caesar who reportedly used it to encrypt military messages and stop them falling into the wrong hands. Caesar's use of this cypher was likely successful as most people during his time weren't literate anyway, and knowledge of cypher use was not widespread.

#  How it works

Caesar cyphers work by shifting all of the letters in the alphabet to the right by a certain number of spaces. So, in the case of a 3 letter shift, 'A' would become 'D', 'B' would become 'E', and 'C' to 'F'.

#  Translator
