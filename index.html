Cyphers are repeatable steps called algorithms that are used to encrypt or decrypt secret messages. A message is entered into a cypher, it is processed through the cypher's algorithm, and then an encrypted message is produced as the output.

#  Cyphers for good

In the First World War, morse code was used to send important military information. To enable these communications, switchboard operators were employed to manage the various lines of connection. In America, the women who worked on these switchboards were known colloquially as ‘Hello Girls’. They had to be fluent in both English and French and highly skilled with the equipment they were using.




#  Cyphers for bad

Through the 1960s and early 1970s, a string of murders were committed by the a serial killer known as The Zodiac Killer. One of the mysteries around this killer was his use of cyphers. On the 1st of August, 1969, three letters were sent to local newspapers, each containing one part of a 408 symbol cryptogram. To this day, only one of the parts has been solved.
